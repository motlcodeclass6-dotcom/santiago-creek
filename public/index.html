What is a Watershed?

 A watershed is an area of land where all the water (rain, rivers,and streams) flows into the same place, like a river, lake, or ocean. For example, when it rains, water runs over the ground and eventually drains into a larger body of water.

 Humans can impact watersheds in many ways. Pollution from trash, chemicals, and oil can wash into rivers through runoff. Also, building cities and roads can increase runoff and reduce clean water, which harms plants, animals, and water quality.

Benefits of Our Watershed

 The Santiago Watershed is important because it provides water for and supports plants and animals. Healthy water systems help keep ecosystems balanced and allow wildlife to survive.

 It also helps manage rain water and reduce flooding. In addition, it provides natural space for people to enjoy, such as parks and outdoor areas.

Field Trip Connection

 During the field trip, we tested the water quality and observed many insect larvae in the creek. These organisms are good signs of water quality because they are typically found in clean, low pollution water. This suggests the water is healthy and clean. The flowing creek and natural canyon habitat also support aquatic life and show that the area has a stable environment.

About Our Watershed

 Our watershed is the Santiago Watershed, located in Orange County, California. Water in this watershed flows through streams and rivers and eventually connects to larger systems like the Santa Ana River and the Pacific ocean.

 The area includes mountains, hills, and urban cities. One important feature is Santiago Creek, which carries water through the region. The climate is mostly dry, but when it rains, water flows quickly over the land, picking up pollution along the way.

 Because there are many roads and buildings, runoff can carry trash, oil, and chemicals into water, which affects water quality and wildlife.

What You Can Do

 You can protect the Santiago Watershed by not littering and properly disposing of trash. We also need to enjoy the natural spaces of this watershed responsibly by staying on trail and respecting the plants and animals. We can also save water by taking shorter showers, turning off the tap that was not in use, and fixing leaks at home. Reducing pollution from our homes includes avoiding pouring chemicals, oils, or waste down drains and using fewer harmful cleaning products.

 You can also help by planting native plants which help reduce runoff because their deep roots absorb water and hold the soil in place. This prevents excess water from carrying pollutants into the creek native plants also require less water and fewer chemicals, which helps keep the water cleaner. These actions help protect wildlife and keep water safe.
